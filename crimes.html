United States Court

Case No. 2024-MOLLY-044

Shitters v. Alex (Defendant)

Charges and Allegations

In the matter of Shitters v. Alex, the Court hereby submits the following list of charges against the Defendant, Alex, based on testimony and evidence presented by the Plaintiffs. Each charge corresponds to specific grievances committed by the Defendant.

Charge 1: Unauthorized Request for Private Information

The Defendant unlawfully sought the private phone number of Molly's mother, constituting an invasion of privacy and a breach of reasonable social boundaries.

Charge 2: Willful Disruption of Relationships

The Defendant intentionally pursued a romantic relationship with Molly’s teammate, resulting in the deterioration of their friendship. This act was executed with the intent to cause distress to the Plaintiff, Molly.

Charge 3: Use of Hateful and Offensive Language

The Defendant is accused of making inappropriate and offensive jokes, including references to sensitive topics and the use of slurs (including the f-slur and n-word). Such behavior caused significant emotional harm to those involved.

Charge 4: Invasion of Privacy

The Defendant acquired and recorded Molly’s home address without her consent, constituting a serious invasion of privacy.

Charge 5: Unwanted Contact and False Representation

The Defendant sent flowers to Molly’s residence without her permission, a gesture that created discomfort and unease. When confronted, the Defendant falsely denied involvement in this act.

Charge 6: False Promises of Rehabilitation

The Defendant repeatedly stated, “I will change,” yet failed to exhibit any meaningful or sustained behavioral improvements, thereby misleading those around him.

Charge 7: Defamation Through Fabrication

The Defendant spread false claims of an intimate relationship with Molly, an act intended to elevate his own reputation at the expense of hers.

Charge 8: Inappropriate Association with Minors

The Defendant engaged in conversations and interactions with individuals in the 7th and 8th grades, despite being of a significantly older age.

Charge 9: Emotional Manipulation Through Threats

The Defendant threatened self-harm as a means of emotionally manipulating others into providing him with attention and support.

Charge 10: Unauthorized Attendance at Events

The Defendant repeatedly invited himself to social gatherings and functions where he was not welcome, disregarding the expressed wishes of the hosts.

Charge 11: Fraudulent Relationship Management

The Defendant pursued multiple romantic interests simultaneously (four or more) and engaged in deceptive practices, lying to these individuals to maintain their attention.

Charge 12: Public Outbursts and Aggressive Behavior

The Defendant exhibited anger issues, including frequent shouting at peers and being removed from games due to disruptive behavior.

Charge 13: Misogynistic Language

The Defendant referred to women as “bitches,” displaying a lack of respect and contributing to a hostile environment.

Charge 14: Refusal to Accept Rejection

The Defendant demonstrated an inability to respect boundaries, responding negatively and persistently to romantic rejection.

Charge 15: Abuse of Group Chat Protocols

Upon being removed from group chats, the Defendant spammed old conversations and added unrelated individuals to the chat in an attempt to justify his behavior and regain attention.

Charge 16: False Representation of Athletic Abilities

The Defendant exaggerated his hockey skills, falsely claiming a higher level of proficiency and accomplishment than reality supported.

Charge 17: Defamation of Women

The Defendant spread false and malicious rumors about women who rejected him, tarnishing their reputations in retaliation.

Conclusion and Request for Remedy

The Plaintiffs request that the Defendant be held accountable for his actions and seek the following remedies:

  • A formal apology to all parties harmed by the Defendant’s behavior.
  • A commitment to behavioral reform with tangible evidence of change.
  • An indefinite restriction from attending events, group chats, or interactions with the Plaintiffs until trust can be reestablished.
